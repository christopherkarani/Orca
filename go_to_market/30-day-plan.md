# 30-Day Drone Customer Acquisition Plan

## Goal

- book 10 customer discovery/demo calls.
- secure 3 serious design partners.
- close 1 paid pilot.
- Book 10 customer discovery/demo calls.
- Secure 3 serious design partners.
- Close 1 paid pilot if possible.
- Collect repeated evidence of pain around agent/autonomy safety, auditability, MAVLink command mediation, SITL evidence, red-team scenarios, and customer/regulatory proof.

## Target Outcomes

- Minimum: 10 conversations.
- Good: 3 design partners.
- Great: 1 paid pilot.
- Excellent: 2+ paid pilots.

## Positioning

Aegis Edge is a simulation/SITL/bench-preparation safety-policy runtime, MAVLink command mediation layer, safety-envelope evaluator, audit/replay system, red-team/fault-injection harness, and safety-case evidence generator for customer evaluation.

It is not a flight controller, approval path, airworthiness proof, live aircraft system, or replacement for the customer's safety process.

## Days 1-3: Proof Bundle And List Prep

Daily actions:

- Day 1: Verify `zig build`, `zig build test`, `aegis-edge docs check`, `aegis-edge demo run geofence-deny`, `aegis-edge proof generate --demo geofence-deny`, and `aegis-edge redteam --ci`.
- Day 1: Assemble links to customer proof docs, pilot overview, known limitations, and sample reports.
- Day 2: Build 50 target accounts from visible public signals only.
- Day 2: Score each account for stack fit, SITL readiness, safety/evidence pain, and risk.
- Day 3: Prepare a 3-5 minute demo recording using the geofence-deny and proof-generation flow.
- Day 3: Finalize founder-led outreach, LinkedIn note, warm-intro ask, discovery script, and follow-up templates.

Expected outputs:

- Verified local proof bundle.
- 50-account target list with no private contact data beyond manually researched business contact fields.
- 30-50 prioritized accounts for first outreach.
- Demo recording or live demo runbook.

## Days 4-10: Founder-Led Outreach And First Calls

Daily actions:

- Send 5-8 high-quality manual outreaches per weekday.
- Ask for 2-4 warm intros per day from relevant founders, robotics engineers, drone operators, investors, or advisors.
- Use one precise pain hypothesis per account.
- Offer a 20-minute call, not a broad product pitch.
- Run first demos when replies convert.
- Record every objection, disqualification reason, and repeated pain theme.

Expected outputs:

- 30-50 founder-led outreaches sent manually.
- 5-10 replies.
- 3-5 calls booked.
- First set of objections around SITL value, existing geofence controls, MAVLink coverage, ROS2, hardware needs, and customer proof.

## Days 11-20: Discovery, Demo, And Pilot Scoping

Daily actions:

- Run technical discovery calls.
- Map each serious account's autonomy command surface.
- Confirm PX4, ArduPilot, MAVLink, ROS2/MAVROS, companion computer, or custom bridge usage.
- Confirm simulation/SITL availability.
- Customize a pilot scope around one autonomy workflow.
- Send pilot proposals to qualified teams.
- Identify 1-3 design partners.

Expected outputs:

- 10 total conversations reached or scheduled.
- 3 serious design-partner candidates.
- 1-3 pilot proposals sent.
- Clear notes on integration blockers and requested scenarios.

## Days 21-30: Pilot Close And Roadmap Evidence

Daily actions:

- Close the highest-fit pilot first.
- Define success criteria, timeline, owners, and data boundaries.
- Schedule kickoff and technical validation.
- Confirm no real-flight or certification expectation.
- Collect product roadmap evidence from objections and requested scenarios.
- Decide whether a customer-specific adapter or policy expansion is justified.

Expected outputs:

- 1 paid pilot closed if possible, or 3 design partners committed.
- Mutual action plan complete.
- Pilot success scorecard complete.
- Product roadmap notes grounded in customer pain.

- Which segment replied fastest?
- Which stack signals predicted strong fit?
- Which pain repeated at least three times?
- Which objections indicate positioning issues?
- Which requests are real pilot scope versus future roadmap?
- Are any claims drifting beyond customer-evaluation evidence?
