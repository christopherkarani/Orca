# Pilot Pricing Guidance

Editable/internal guidance. This is editable/internal guidance. Do not publish price ranges without founder approval.

## Free Design Partner vs Paid Pilot

free design partner vs paid pilot

Free design partner:

- Use when the logo, technical feedback, or roadmap access is unusually valuable.
- Keep duration and deliverables smaller than a paid pilot.
- Require a named technical owner and feedback commitment.

Paid pilot:

- Use when the customer has real urgency, simulator readiness, and budget.
- Tie payment to a bounded deliverable, not vague advisory work.
- Require a mutual action plan.

## Suggested Price Ranges

- First design partners: $2.5k-$10k.
- Later pilots: $10k-$25k.
- Enterprise/custom pilot: custom.

## When To Discount

- Strong ICP fit.
- Fast access to simulator and command examples.
- Clear design-partner feedback.
- Potential reference if pilot succeeds.

## When Not To Discount

- Customer wants broad custom work.
- Customer cannot provide technical owner.
- Customer wants evidence beyond evaluation scope.
- Customer requires unusual security/legal overhead.

## What Qualifies A Paid Pilot

- One autonomy workflow.
- Defined command surface.
- Simulation/SITL or fake-adapter path.
- Clear success criteria.
- Named buyer or budget owner.
- Review meeting scheduled before kickoff.

## What To Avoid Promising

what to avoid promising

- Approval outcomes.
- Aircraft validation.
- Full command coverage.
- Custom adapter delivery without scope.
- Field deployment.
- Guaranteed customer acceptance.

## Scope Tightly

- One workflow.
- One environment.
- One policy baseline.
- One scenario list.
- One report package.

## Payment Milestones

- 50% at kickoff, 50% on report delivery.
- Or 100% upfront for small design-partner pilots.
- Enterprise/custom pilots require separate commercial review.
