# What Aegis Edge Demonstrates In Fixtures

In checked-in fake-adapter, SITL, and bench-preparation fixtures, Aegis Edge can demonstrate:

- Fixture commands are denied according to policy when they violate the modeled safety envelope.
- Commands are evaluated against a safety envelope.
- Mission items can be checked against geofence and altitude policy.
- Operator approvals are scoped, local, and auditable.
- Stale telemetry can trigger conservative behavior.
- Runtime health findings are logged.
- Telemetry/data egress can be classified and denied.
- Audit logs are replayable and tamper-evident through hash-chain verification.
- Safety-case reports can summarize fixture evidence, findings, decisions, limitations, provenance, and policy hash.

These are product-evaluation claims for the local evidence corpus, not real-flight claims.
